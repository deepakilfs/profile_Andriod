Working Hours

Policy

The policy regulates the working timings for all team members to enable coordination and teamwork.

Eligibility

The policy applies to all permanent employees, temporary employees and management trainees associated with IETS.

Terms & Conditions
  • The Competent Authority may amend or change the working timings to promote operational flexibilities.
  • All entries post 15 minutes from the reporting time will considered as Late Coming.
  • All employees should carry their identity cards daily to provide access for entry and exit from the office.
  • Employees shall immediately report the loss or damage of the card to the HR Department. A replacement fee of Rs.500 will be charged to the employee.
  • Wherever attendance is recorded by the biometric system. All employees need to record their attendance in the system. All late comings will be consolidated at the end of the month by HR department and local leave administrator at the branches to deduct appropriate amounts against late timings from the subsequent month’s payroll.
  • In case of unforeseen delays or absence from work, employees shall inform the Superior and update the attendance register accordingly. Failure to inform the superior may lead to late coming / Absence in the attendance register.
  • In case employees have to attend early morning meetings outside office premises, he/ she may directly attend the meeting post informing and taking approval of the Superior in advance. Failure to inform the superior may lead to late coming/ absence in the attendance register
  • For remote location offices, where local admin. Departments are not available, employees must inform the respective superiors/ branch heads and the centralized Admin department in Mumbai office on a daily basis using SMS to ensure recording of attendance. Failure to do so may result in late coming/ absence in the attendance register. In case of unforeseen exigencies or early morning meetings, employees must telephonically inform / SMS the superiors.
